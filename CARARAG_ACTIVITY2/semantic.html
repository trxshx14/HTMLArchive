Health Benefits of Beets

WHAT ARE BEETS?

Beets or beetroots are a root vegetable also known as Beta vulgaris ruba or red beetroot. Vibrant red or gold beetroot has been planted everywhere from ancient Mediterranean to George Washington's Mount Vernon.

The leaves and roots are packed with nutrition, including antioxidants that fight cell damage and reduce the risk of heart disease. They're one of the few vegetables that contain betalains, a powerful antioxidant that gives beets their vibrant color. Betalains reduce inflammation and may help protect against cancer and other diseases.

5 BEETROOT BENEFITS

1. Prevent heart disease and stroke

Beets are rich in nitrates, which can help relax blood vessels, lower blood pressure, and improve circulation. This reduces the risk of heart disease and stroke.

2. Lower blood pressure

The nitrates in beets can lower blood pressure by dilating blood vessels and increasing blood flow. This helps regulate blood pressure levels.

3. Boost your immune system

Beets contain vitamin C, which boosts the immune system and helps the body fight off infections and diseases.

4. May prevent anemia

Beets are a good source of iron and folate, which are essential for red blood cell production. Consuming beets may help prevent and treat anemia.

5. May promote weight loss

Beets are low in calories and high in fiber, making them a filling and nutritious addition to weight loss diets. The fiber in beets promotes satiety and helps regulate appetite.
